## Brand Identity

### Brand Voice

The voice is **knowledgeable but approachable** ‚Äî like a neighbor who happens to be really good at this. Not corporate, not overly casual. Think "enthusiast who takes their craft seriously but doesn't take themselves too seriously."

**Do:**
- Use first person ("I just finished sequencing..." or "We're running 190 universes this year")
- Share genuine excitement about the hobby
- Be helpful and generous with knowledge
- Show the work ‚Äî behind the scenes, the process, the failures
- Be conversational and warm
- Use humor when it's natural (not forced)

**Don't:**
- Sound like a corporate social media account
- Use excessive emojis (one per post max, zero is fine)
- Use hashtag walls (3‚Äì5 targeted hashtags max)
- Be salesy in every post
- Talk down to beginners
- Use generic motivational language ("Living our best lights life!")
- Overuse exclamation marks

**Tone calibration examples:**

## Content Strategy

### The 80/20 Rule

80% of posts should be **value, personality, or community** content. 20% can be **promotional** (new sequences, sales, product links). This ratio is critical in hobby communities where hard selling gets you unfollowed or labeled as spam.

### Content Pillars

Each post should map to one of these five pillars:

| Pillar | % of content | Purpose | Examples |
|--------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Show Off** | 30% | Display footage, the wow factor | Full show clips, single-song videos, drone shots, time-lapses, viewer reactions |
| **Behind the Scenes** | 25% | Build connection and credibility | Sequencing screen recordings, setup/teardown, problem-solving, xLights screenshots, wiring photos |
| **Knowledge Share** | 20% | Establish expertise, help community | Quick tips, prop reviews, "how I did this effect," controller comparisons, lessons learned |
| **Community** | 10% | Build relationships | Reposting other displays, responding to community challenges, holiday-themed content, polls |
| **Product** | 15% | Drive sequence sales | New sequence announcements, preview clips, free sequence drops, sale events |

### Content Calendar Rhythm

Holiday lighting has a strong seasonal cycle. Content strategy shifts dramatically across the year.

**January‚ÄìMarch (Off-season / Planning)**
- 1‚Äì2 posts per week
- Focus: Behind the scenes (planning next season), knowledge share (gear reviews, "what I learned"), community engagement
- Sequence content: "Building for next year" teasers, free sequence drops to build catalog awareness

**April‚ÄìJune (Build season)**
- 2‚Äì3 posts per week
- Focus: Behind the scenes (new props arriving, building/wiring), knowledge share (how-to content)
- Sequence content: Work-in-progress clips, "sequencing this right now" posts

**July‚ÄìSeptember (Peak build + sequencing)**
- 3‚Äì4 posts per week, increasing toward September
- Focus: Heavy behind-the-scenes (setup progress), show-off (test runs), product (new sequences dropping)
- This is when the xLights community is most active and buying sequences

**October (Halloween show live)**
- 4‚Äì5 posts per week
- Focus: Show-off (live show footage every few days), product (Halloween sequences), community (viewer reactions, visitor photos)
- Peak engagement period for Halloween displays

**November (Transition + Christmas prep)**
- 3‚Äì4 posts per week
- Focus: Behind the scenes (changeover from Halloween to Christmas), product (Christmas sequences), teasers

**December (Christmas show live)**
- 4‚Äì5 posts per week
- Focus: Show-off (live show footage), product (Christmas sequences), community (visitor stories, neighborhood reactions)
- Highest engagement period of the year

**Posting times (Central Time):**
- Facebook: 7‚Äì9 PM weekdays, 10 AM‚Äì12 PM weekends
- Instagram: 6‚Äì8 PM weekdays, 9‚Äì11 AM weekends
- YouTube: Friday or Saturday afternoon (allows weekend viewing)

## Community Engagement Rules

### Facebook Groups

This is the highest-leverage activity and the easiest to mess up.

**Phase 1 ‚Äî Lurk and learn (Weeks 1‚Äì2):**
- Join key groups: "xLights ‚Äî pair/share," regional lighting groups, "Holiday Lighting & Decorating"
- Read posts, understand the culture, identify regular contributors and moderators
- Do NOT post anything promotional

**Phase 2 ‚Äî Be helpful (Weeks 3‚Äì6):**
- Answer questions you genuinely know the answer to (controller setup, effect techniques, prop recommendations)
- Comment on other people's display posts with specific, genuine compliments ("That arch timing at 0:42 is butter")
- Share a tip or lesson learned from your own experience
- Let people discover your profile/page naturally through your comments

**Phase 3 ‚Äî Natural promotion (Week 7+):**
- When someone asks "where do you get sequences?" ‚Äî now you can mention yours naturally
- Share your own display footage when relevant (not out of context)
- If asked directly about your setup, link to your site
- NEVER start a post with "Check out my new sequence!" in someone else's group

**Things that get you labeled as spam (avoid at all costs):**
- Posting product links in groups within your first month
- Posting the same content across multiple groups on the same day
- Responding to every "where to buy sequences" post with your link
- Creating posts that are thinly veiled ads ("Just wanted to share this awesome sequence I made...")
- Tagging your page or using your brand name excessively in comments

### Responding to Mentions and Tags

- Respond to every tag, mention, or share within 24 hours
- Be genuine and grateful, not canned ("Thanks for checking out the show!" not "Thanks for your support! üôè")
- If someone posts video of your show, ask permission to reshare and credit them
- If someone has a negative comment or complaint, respond calmly and take it to DM if needed

### Handling Competitor Comparisons

- Never trash other sequence vendors
- If someone compares you to xTreme, Magical, etc., acknowledge the other vendor positively and differentiate on what makes you different (design, pricing transparency, the actual show)
- "Ron at xTreme makes great stuff ‚Äî we're just coming at it from a different angle. All our sequences are tested on a live 35k pixel display."

## Promotional Content Guidelines

### New Sequence Launch

Standard launch sequence across platforms:

1. **Teaser (3‚Äì5 days before):** 10-second clip or xLights screenshot. "Something new coming this week..." No song name yet.
2. **Launch day:**
   - YouTube: Full preview video
   - Instagram: Reel (best 30‚Äì60 seconds) + Story with link
   - Facebook: Native video upload + link in comments
3. **Follow-up (2‚Äì3 days later):** Behind-the-scenes post about making the sequence. What was tricky, what you're proud of, how long it took.

### Free Sequence Drops

Free sequences are your best marketing tool. They lower the barrier to trying your work and build goodwill.

- Announce with slightly more excitement than paid sequences ‚Äî generosity is attractive
- "Dropping this one for free because [reason ‚Äî it's a classic, it was a fun experiment, happy holidays]"
- Always link to the full catalog so people discover paid sequences organically

### Sale Events

Keep sales rare and meaningful. This community is small enough that constant discounting erodes perceived value.

- Black Friday / seasonal sales are expected and fine
- "Buy 3 get 1 free" or percentage discounts, not dramatic markdowns
- Frame sales around value, not desperation

1. **Always ask which platform** if not specified ‚Äî the approach differs significantly between Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram
2. **Draft complete posts** ‚Äî include the caption/description, suggested hashtags, and posting notes (time, any cross-posting)
